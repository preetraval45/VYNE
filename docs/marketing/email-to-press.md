Cold-outbound to journalists + newsletters in the productivity + AI tooling beat. Send **Monday or Tuesday morning ET** of launch week, never Friday. Personalize the subject + first sentence; everything below the fold can be a template.

## Subject line (first impression — 60 char max)

A/B test these three on different recipients:

1. **Show: an AI that runs my whole company in one workspace**
2. **Built an AI workspace replacing 8 SaaS tools (Show HN today)**
3. **[Their name] — building the AI workspace that kills tool sprawl**

Pick #3 for VC outreach (personalized hook works), #1 for press, #2 for newsletters.

## Customizations per audience

**For TC / Verge / The Information** — lead with the contrarian thesis ("teams don't need 15 tools, they need 1 AI that knows about everything"). They love a thesis-driven piece.

**For Lenny / Pragmatic Engineer** — lead with the receipts (250K LOC solo, 3 months, AI dev tooling did 60% of the typing). They reward depth + transparency.

**For VCs** — DM not email. X DM > LinkedIn > email. Lead with the metric they care about (which depends on the VC — Tavel cares about retention, Chen cares about growth loops).

**For newsletters (Lenny etc)** — offer the exclusive: "happy to write a first-person piece on the build for your newsletter, embargoed to your publish date." Newsletter writers love guest posts that don't cost them research time.

---

## What NOT to send

- A 2-page deck. Press doesn't read decks. Send a tight email + a single working link.
- A YouTube video. They'll watch the first 5 seconds. Better: a 30-second loop GIF embedded inline.
- "Exclusive" claims to multiple recipients. Send to ONE outlet exclusive at a time, with a 24h embargo. If they pass, move to the next.
- The full press release. The release is for after they say yes — at the email stage, send the 3-paragraph pitch above.

---

## Follow-up cadence

- **+24h after first email**: bump only if no reply. ONE bump, no more. "Hi — flagging this in case it landed in spam."
- **+1 week**: if still no reply, drop it. Move on.
- **Replies asking questions**: respond within 30 minutes during business hours. Press operate on tight deadlines; latency loses the piece.
- **Replies asking for a call**: book it within 48 hours. The hot pitch loses heat fast.

---

## Tracking

Drop every email into a single Notion / Airtable / spreadsheet so you don't double-pitch. Columns: outlet, name, date sent, follow-up date, status (no-reply / declined / open / scheduled / published). Seven outlets a day for a week = 35 pitches; expect 5-15% reply rate, 1-3 published pieces in the first 30 days. That's a successful press launch.
